Our community-based initiatives act as entry points for education, where we provide training courses to educate Batwa people about their legal entitlement to an education and foster inclusion. About Us

Small Ideas, Big Revolutions

Dear Friends,
Nawe Niwige was founded to address the educational requirements of marginalized, impoverished young Indigenous Batwa people in Burundi, starting in Kirundo Province.

Four young Indigenous Batwa from different universities in Rwanda started the program after observing how their younger siblings struggle to access education owing to a lack of school materials and fees. The program began by supporting twenty Batwa children in primary school using the little money we get on scholarships.

We present an interwoven collection of initiatives emphasizing social inclusion, education support, and promoting indigenous self-awareness. We collaborate with village elders and local authorities to encourage and support young Batwa people to enroll in schools. We also work with school administrators to monitor the learning environment of Batwa students.

Our dedication to our community

Our Mission

Promote access to quality education for young, marginalized indigenous people (Batwa) in the Kirundo province of Burundi by reducing school dropouts through providing school materials and fees for Batwa youth, and working with local authorities to ensure that every kid in the Batwa community gets access to formal, basic education.

Learn More

Our Vision

Aspire to unlock the potential of young indigenous people by fostering self-awareness, a better understanding of their rights, and enriching their leadership skills through easy access to high education; thus creating bright leaders for a bright future.

Learn More

Our Objectives

Our objectives aim at transforming the lives of the Batwa people by reducing 40% of illiteracy in the Batwa community through providing educational opportunities by 2035, focusing on an improved understanding of human rights, access to primary & high education, means of subsistence, healthcare education, gender, environment, and culture.
